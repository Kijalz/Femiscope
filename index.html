About Us

We are Ready to Help and Mentor Adolescent Girls

At our core, we believe in the power of mentorship to change lives, especially for adolescent girls in underprivileged and marginalized communities. These young girls face a multitude of social injustices that expose them to harmful cultural practices such as gender-based violence (GBV), female genital mutilation (FGM), early pregnancies, early marriages, and challenges related to sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR). Our mission is to provide a safe, supportive space for these girls, where they can find guidance, encouragement, and resources to overcome these barriers. We use an intersectional approach to identify their unique challenges and design bespoke programs that address their specific needs. By fostering a sense of community and empowerment, we aim to help them develop a positive self-image, achieve higher aspirations, and ultimately break the cycle of vulnerability. Through mentorship, educational initiatives, and advocacy, we are dedicated to creating a world where every girl has the opportunity to reach her full potential. We invite you to join us in this transformative journey, where together, we can make a lasting impact on the lives of these young girls. Inspirational Story

In 2022, I was inspired to utilise mentorship as a tool to bridge the existing gender gaps in education among adolescent girls. After having multiple conversations with a few adolescents in my community, it was clear that most of the girls lacked the knowledge or idea of how to align their aspirations and dreams with what they were studying in school. Most of them had unclear ambitions coupled with misleading/misguided road maps that would lead them further from their aspirations, as such, I was inspired to stand in the gap and offer first-hand mentorship based on my experiences â€“ both my successes and failures.

Empowering Adolescent Girls: The Launch of Femiscope Initiative in Githurai 44

On March 30th, a significant step towards empowering adolescent girls took place in Githurai 44, Nairobi, as the Femiscope Initiative was officially launched at Candlelight Academy. Spearheaded by Founder and CEO Sally Ndwiga, the event gathered 25 attendees, including adolescents, educators, and community members dedicated to uplifting young women. The event featured insightful discussions and presentations, including a talk by East African Champion Moses Gichuho, who shared best practices in mentoring adolescents. Sally Ndwiga led a group activity on the Intersectionality Approach, helping participants understand the complex layers of identity that shape adolescents and equipping them with strategies to address their challenges. The success of the event led to invitations for Sally to engage with more adolescents and speak at future youth events at Candlelight Academy, highlighting the initiativeâ€™s impact. Gratitude was extended to the One Campaign for their collaboration, which was pivotal in the event's success. Looking ahead, the Femiscope Initiative aims to make a lasting difference in the lives of adolescent girls in Githurai 44 and beyond.
